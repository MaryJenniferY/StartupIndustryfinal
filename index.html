Financial Resources

Availability of finance is critical for the startups and is always a problem to get sufficient amounts. A number of finance options ranging from family members, friends, loans, grants, angel funding, venture capitalists, crowd funding etc are available. The requirement starts increasing as the business progresses. Scaling of business requires timely infusion of capital. Proper cash management is critical for the success of the startups

Many startups need loans, and there are plenty of options to choose from, depending on your business niche and the country where you live. In general, it is best to ask your family members and friends if they can lend you some cash. If they are not able to, research the potential options, and choose the best solution

Business Plan & Product Validation

People often underestimate the fact that they have to write a thorough business plan before getting into the business. The strategy has to cover all segments of business in order to be useful and successful. Some of the crucial areas are funding, staff, suppliers, marketing, and sales. Also, each segment has to be realistic and has to follow the exact budget limits. Startups which do not have the right mentorship to accomplish this part of the process consider hiring business advisors to help you with creating a good business plan.

It’s amazing how many startups falter because they “forgot” to plan. Or maybe they really did plan, but they just didn’t cover all the bases. Key areas like sales, development, staffing, skills shortage, and funding aren’t afterthoughts. They should all be a part of your business plan right from the beginning.

I've seen many startups rush into development of an idea before truly understanding the requirements for a successful product and before validating the financial viability of the opportunity. Whenever someone approaches me for tips or advice on how to start a new company, I always ask them three things:

How well do they know the problem or industry?

Why would anyone trust them to deliver a solution for the problem?

How will they monetize, and if there's a valid monetization plan, will it be enough to cover the expense of developing and operating the solution?

Competitors

No matter how great a product or services is, it’s a crowded marketplace.
And it’s growing all the time: you won’t be the new kid on the block for long, and new rivals can quickly alter the playing field.
When you’re building a startup, it can be tempting to worry about competitors—especially the big companies running your industry.
But you have to focus on what you can control: moving quickly and looking ahead. Why? Because worrying about competition is an unnecessary distraction and makes you paranoid for no good reason.

Time Management

In startups as in life, there’s never enough time. There are a million and one decisions to be made and only 24 hours in a day.
Startups should focus on eliminating or minimizing distractions — anything that gets in the way of running your business.
Focus your time and energy on the most impactful things. Will this make the boat go faster? –Ben Hunts Davis That is to say, will this decision or action have significant, measurable, positive results that will help you hit your targets?

Creating Presence & Awareness

Startups fail due to lack of attention to limitations in the markets. The environment for a startup is usually more difficult than for an established firm due to uniqueness of the product. The situation is more difficult for a new product as the startup has to build everything from scratch.

Businesses leverage digital channels likewise – search engines, emails, social media, and their websites so as to connect with more and more customers. In this modern scenario, we all know the importance of the digital market, yet many entrepreneurs do not have a proper plan to take the edge of potential opportunities in this digital world. It has evaluated that an effective digital marketing strategy won’t only assist in making corrective business decisions but also renders a planned approach in order to make a brand successful.

Lack of a good branding strategy & Neglecting marketing and sales

Absence of an effective branding strategy is another issue that prevents startups from flourishing at a faster pace. Hemant Arora, Business Head-Branded Content, Times Network opines that :

” Branding demands paramount attention as it gives an identity and occupies a space in the consumer minds”

Are you putting enough resources into marketing and sales? Some startups think they can ignore those two functions completely and hope that word of mouth will be enough. Or if they’re a SaaS (Software as Service) company, they might believe that sales will grow organically online, and that IRL sales and marketing teams aren’t needed. But it’s a false economy to put your faith in customers discovering you unless you make a concerted effort to grow them with a proper structured plan to promote your startup.

FAILURE (Something Nobody Talks About)

No, founders don't repay investors if a startup fails. The investor takes the risk, owns a share in the company, and loses the money if the startup fails and that share loses value. If the founders owe the money, that would have been debt, not investment. ... The key is the difference between investment and debt.
Data from the BLS shows that approximately 20% of new businesses fail during the first two years of being open, 45% during the first five years, and 65% during the first 10 years. Only 25% of new businesses make it to 15 years or more.
Two to three years is the standard estimation for how long it takes a business to be profitable. That said, each startup has different initial costs and ways of measuring profit. A business could become profitable immediately or take three years or longer to make money.
